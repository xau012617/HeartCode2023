What is Online Flaming?

Online flaming refers to the act of posting hostile, aggressive, or inflammatory messages on the internet, often with the intent to provoke or offend others. It typically occurs in online discussions, forums, or social media platforms and can lead to heated exchanges and conflict among users.

Victim's Emotional State

    Sadness
    Anger
    Fear
    Loneliness
    Hurt

This can cause the victim to have a low-self esteem.

Platforms involved

TikTok: Some users may leave hostile or offensive comments on videos, engaging in heated arguments in the comment section, or creating and sharing videos with the purpose of provoking or attacking others.

Gaming: Online flaming in gaming involves players posting aggressive or insulting messages to provoke others during gameplay. This can include derogatory comments about opponents' skills, personal attacks, or offensive language. Examples include trash-talking in chat, targeting specific players with insults, or engaging in heated arguments that disrupt the gaming experience. Such behavior often escalates tensions and negatively impacts the overall enjoyment of the gaming community.

Possible Scenario

Player A is new to the game. He plays in a team game and causes the team to lose the game. Players B and C starts cursing vulgarities at Player A through the in-game chat.

How To Spot Online Flaming

    Insults and Offensive Language: Pay attention to the use of insults, offensive language, or derogatory remarks directed at others.
    Personal Attacks: Identify comments or messages that personally attack an individual rather than addressing the topic at hand.
    Excessive Caps Lock or Punctuation: Excessive use of capital letters, exclamation points, or other punctuation can indicate heightened emotions and potential flaming.

How To Prevent Online Flaming

    Think Before You Type: Remind yourself to think before posting comments. Consider the potential impact of your words and whether they contribute positively to the conversation.
    Use Humour Positively: If humour is involved, ensure it is positive and inclusive rather than at the expense of others. Avoid sarcasm or jokes that may be interpreted as offensive.
    Report Inappropriate Behavior: Familiarize yourself with the reporting features on online platforms and use them to report instances of online flaming or harassment. This helps maintain a safer community.
